Cell Phone Use While Driving: Dangers and Benefits

Discover More

Dangers

Driving while using a cell phone is considered to be one of the riskiest driving behaviors because it requires the driver to become visually and mentally distracted. The best way to minimize cell phone usage and its potential hazards, while driving is to educate people of its dangers:

  • A driver's eyes are off the road for 4.6 seconds when sending or receiving a text message, according to a study by the Virginia Tech Transportation Institute (VTTI).
  • The National Safety Council estimates that cell phone use while driving caused about 1.5 million car accidents in 2017.
  • According to the Virginia Tech Transportation Institute, texting while driving is six times more likely to cause an auto crash than driving when intoxicated.

Benefits

Cell phones have gotten a bad rap because of the increase of cell phone-related car accidents. But there are numerous pros that support use of cell phones while driving:

  • Cell phones allow users to call for directions when they cannot find their destinations.
  • Cell phones also allow users to immediately call the police to report suspicious actions they see while driving.
  • Cell phone cameras allow drivers to take pictures of license plate nubers in the event of a hit or run.
  • Cell phones allow users to call for immediate roadside assistance.
